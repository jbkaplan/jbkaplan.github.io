Week 6:

Ruby-Classes

March 20, 2016

Ruby classes are very useful. They are great way for organizing objects. Classes are like a blueprint, they contain methods attached to the class that can be executed. Classes make our methods easier to access, they form hierarcy, and form objects. Think of dice, a die can have 6 sides so when it is rolled, either one of the 6 sides can show up.

In action, our Dice class can look like this:

Let's break a few things down here.

First, we have our methods contained in one class Die, this will be our blueprint. We start our class through an initialize method, and here we created that method which takes an argument (sides). This initialize method is used to receive our argument so that it can begin a method. In our initialize method, we also define an ArgumentError to be brought up if the sides we define on our Die is not > 1, because, well, what's the point in rolling a 1 sided die?!

Next, have our roll method which will use a random method to simulate a real rolled die, and return that side.

To call our class we start with a variable to define our class die = Die.new(6) (start our method to roll a 6 sided die), and then we can use that to call our other 2 methods for die.sides (return our 6 sides we defined), and die.roll (generate a random roll).
